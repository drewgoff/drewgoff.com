SIGMA Recovery

Idle asset inventory software

The Problem

Lot's of large scale companies have factories that have idle manufacturing equipment gathering dust. A lot of these companies don't realize the value of their idle equipment.

The Solution:

Research

We wanted to design software that gives our customers access to their idle equipment inventory. The software would allow the customer to see the value of their idle assets and allow us to help them Redeploy, Recondition, Resell or Recycle their equipment.



So we wanted the dashboard to be the first screen that the customer see when first logging in.

The Solution:

Design

Monochromatic design is what inspired SIGMA Recovery's design. I had done a little bit of monochromatic design at OneMain with the use of blues on blues. I was curious to use grey's on grey's this time with an accent green color that would symbolize recycling.



The trick I had learned is to have your darkest grey at the bottom and then build up and go lighter as each level increased.

The Solution:

Coding

I started coding the prototype of SIGMA Recovery when I was designing the marketing. I actually did almost all of the coding at home in the evenings and weekends.



When I presented it to the CEO, he got excited and decided to include the entire programming team on it. We had six weeks to build the software from the ground up.



Luckily I had the interface coded in HTML 5, SCSS (CSS 3), SVG and JavaScript. When we put the entire engineering team on it, we transferred it into React, Typescript, C# and SQL.
