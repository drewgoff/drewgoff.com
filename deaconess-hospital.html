Deaconess Riley Childrens Hospital

Interior Designer (Photography)

The Problem

The Riley children’s hospital is in Indianapolis. Depending on traffic that is four hours away from Evansville. So it was important to create a children’s hospital that functioned as an extension to the Riley’s children hospital. A hospital where the doctors from Indianapolis could come to Evansville and see children that might be too sick to travel that far.

Now that we have the hospital built… now it has to be designed.

Nature photo sessions

Wesselman Woods

The Solution:

Research

What we found from interviewing patients and examining other successful children’s hospitals. A children’s hospital has to be designed in a way that it promotes healing and makes the patients feel connected to their community. This is especially true of children who had longer stays at the hospital due to more severe illinesses.

In order to engage in the child like spirit that promotes healing, we had to get away from the sterile blues and whites of the typical hospitals. We had to study color theory, we had to study child psychology, we had to put our selves in the shoes of these children, so we could see life from a child’s perspective.

Once we put the research all together, the plan appeared. We were going to call our designs “Paths Of Nature”. We could showcase the natural beauty of the outdoor activites that are a apart of Evansville and southern Indiana.

This plan was two fold. The first fold, was that we would be connecting these patients to their community by reminding them of the beauty that their community offers.

The second fold, is that we would be bringing the outside to the inside. This would make these children not feel like they are just in sterile hospital. We could bring the colors of nature and blend them in a way that felt childlike and filled with imagination.

Now that we had a plan, Tara and I divided our roles up. She took care of the designs and I was to do the large scale photography.

Nature photo sessions

Angel Mounds

The Solution:

Design

So I mapped out the most beautiful places in Evansville and it’s surrounding areas. The four month journey began. One of the things that separated our designs from many other children’s hospitals. Was that we were going to make enormous prints of the photography to make the children feel like they were in the environments.

These photos were photographed at extremely high megapixels. Our Angel Mounds photo was printed 30 foot wide by 15 feet tall. The Angel Mounds photo was a whopping 253 megapixels. Even our Wesselman Woods photo that was printed 16 feet wide by 10 feet tall was 50 megapixels.

The Result

We had our grand opening and their were hundreds of people that attended. My friends that came with me were surprised that I was not boosting about my work. Instead I walked around and watched how the children interacted with the environment.

To my delight, I watched a little boy go over the angel mounds 30 foot printed photo. The little boy danced around the backdrop and told his mother in delight “Mommy it’s like I am at the Angel Mounds”.
